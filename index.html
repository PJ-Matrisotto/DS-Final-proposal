Permutation & Combination Calculator

A permutation is an arrangement of objects where the order matters, while a combination is a selection of objects

where the order does not matter. For example, picking a president, vice president, and treasurer is a permutation

because the order of selection creates different outcomes, whereas choosing three committee members is a combination

because the order of selection does not change the group.

Enter values for N and R to calculate permutations and combinations.
